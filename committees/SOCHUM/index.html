SOCHUM

Agenda: "Ensuring the safety of asylum seekers"

Every day, people are forced to flee their homes due to war, violence or persecution. Once someone crosses an International border, they have the right to “Seek Asylum” in order to obtain protection in the country where they have found refuge. Worldwide there are 32.5 million refugees. Refugees are people who have been able to obtain asylum which means that they have the right to stay and be protected like other citizens in their country of refuge. Before becoming refugees, they are asylum seekers and unfortunately there are many situations in the world where people are restricted from seeking asylum. Ensuring the safety and well-being of asylum seekers is a paramount commitment that transcends borders and speaks to the very essence of our shared humanity. Asylum seekers might face pushbacks at borders, discrimination, trafficking and arbitrary detention.

In a world rife with challenges, it becomes our collective duty to establish robust systems that guarantee the protection of those seeking refuge from perilous circumstances. The SOCHUM recognizes the key factors to be "No pushback at borders", "No discrimination", "Safe access", "Humane treatment" and aims to ensure these issues are resolved through meaningful debate at the conference.

The SOCHUM committee is also participating in the UNHCR Refugee Challenge.
